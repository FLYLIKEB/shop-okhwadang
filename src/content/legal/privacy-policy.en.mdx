# Ockhwadang Privacy Policy

Seoro International (the "Company") establishes and publicly discloses this Privacy Policy pursuant to Article 30 of the Korean Personal Information Protection Act ("PIPA") to protect personal information of data subjects and to promptly and smoothly handle any related grievances.

Effective Date 2026-04-20
Last Revised 2026-04-20

## Article 1. Purposes of Processing Personal Information

The Company processes personal information for the following purposes. Personal information being processed will not be used for purposes other than those specified below, and separate consent will be obtained in accordance with Article 18 of PIPA if the purposes of use are changed.

- Member management — Member identification, verification, fraud prevention, and complaint handling
- Service provision — Order processing, payment, and delivery of teapots, pu-erh tea, and tea ware; customer support
- Personalization and service improvement — Usage statistics, analysis, new service development, and personalized product recommendations
- Marketing and advertising — Event and promotion notices, targeted advertising (with separate consent)

## Article 2. Categories of Personal Information Processed

The Company processes the following categories of personal information.

Required Items
- Name — Member identification and order verification
- Email — Login, order confirmation, and service notifications
- Phone number — Order confirmation and delivery contact
- Address (delivery) — Product shipping

Optional Items
- Marketing consent information — Event and promotion notifications

Automatically Collected Items

Information that may be automatically generated and collected during service use, including IP addresses, cookies, MAC addresses, service usage records, visit records, and fraudulent use records. For mobile app users, advertising identifiers (ADID/IDFA) and device information (OS, model, carrier) may additionally be collected.

Social Login Items

Kakao Login: nickname, email (if provided), profile image (if provided)
Google Login: name, email, profile image

## Article 3. Retention and Use Period of Personal Information

The Company processes and retains personal information within the retention/use period specified by applicable laws or consented to by data subjects at the time of collection.

## Article 6. Destruction Procedure and Method

When personal information becomes unnecessary due to expiration of retention periods or achievement of processing purposes, the Company destroys such information without delay. Destruction requires approval from the Data Protection Officer. Electronic files are permanently deleted using methods that prevent recovery; paper documents are shredded or incinerated. Information required to be retained under law is segregated in separate databases or storage locations.

## Article 8. Automatic Collection Devices (Cookies)

The Company uses cookies to provide personalized services by storing and retrieving usage information. Cookies assist in analyzing visit frequency, dwell time, user preferences, interests, and event participation.

How to Refuse Cookies

- Chrome — Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
- Safari — Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
- Edge — Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Manage cookies and site data
- Firefox — Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
- iOS — Settings > Privacy & Security > Tracking > Allow Apps to Request to Track
- Android — Settings > Privacy > Ads > Reset advertising ID

Refusal of cookies may limit use of personalized services.

Behavioral Information for Targeted Advertising

| Collector | Purpose | Items | Retention | Country |
|-----------|---------|-------|-----------|---------|
| Google LLC (Google Analytics) | Service analytics and targeted advertising | Cookies, visit records, device info | 26 months | USA |

Opt-out is available through the methods above and at the Cookie Settings page (/cookie-settings).

## Article 9. Security Measures

The Company implements administrative, technical, and physical measures to ensure the security of personal information. Administrative measures include internal management plans, minimization and training of personnel handling personal information, and regular self-audits. Technical measures include access privilege management, retention of access logs, encryption (one-way hashing for passwords, AES-256 for critical data), and operation of intrusion prevention and detection systems. Physical measures include access control to computer rooms and data storage areas.
